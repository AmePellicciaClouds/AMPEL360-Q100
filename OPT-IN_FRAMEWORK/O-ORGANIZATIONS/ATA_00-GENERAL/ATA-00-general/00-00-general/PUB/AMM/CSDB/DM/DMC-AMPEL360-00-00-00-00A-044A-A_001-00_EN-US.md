## Safety Labeling Standard Overview

This Data Module defines the safety labeling standards for the AMPEL360 Q100 
                    BWB hydrogen-hybrid aircraft program. It establishes a consistent hierarchy 
                    of warnings, cautions, and notes with specific focus on hydrogen-related 
                    hazards unique to the Q100 propulsion system.

Purpose: To ensure consistent 
                    communication of safety-critical information across all technical publications, 
                    maintenance procedures, and operational documents.

Scope: Covers all safety message 
                    types, hydrogen-specific hazard symbols, typography standards, color coding, 
                    and S1000D markup requirements.


## Severity Hierarchy

The Q100 program uses a three-level severity hierarchy based on ISO 3864 
                    and ANSI Z535.4 standards:


### WARNING

Definition: Indicates a hazard 
                        with a medium level of risk which, if not avoided, could result in death 
                        or serious injury.

Usage Criteria:

Potential for fatality or permanent injury
                            Immediate danger to personnel
                            High-energy hazards (electrical, mechanical, thermal)
                            Hazardous material exposure

Visual Format:



| Element | Specification |
| --- | --- |
| Signal Word | WARNING (all capitals) |
| Color | Orange background (#FF6600), Black text |
| Symbol | Triangular hazard symbol with exclamation mark |
| Font Weight | Bold for signal word, regular for message |


Q100-Specific Examples:

warning
                            
                                CRYOGENIC BURN HAZARD. Liquid hydrogen at -253°C (-423°F). 
                                Contact with skin causes severe frostbite and tissue damage. Wear 
                                insulated cryogenic gloves and face shield when handling LH₂ lines 
                                and fittings.

warning
                            
                                FLAMMABLE GAS HAZARD. Hydrogen has wide flammability range 
                                (4-75% in air). Invisible flame. Ensure adequate ventilation. No 
                                ignition sources within 25 feet of venting operations. Use hydrogen 
                                detector before entering enclosed spaces.

warning
                            
                                HIGH PRESSURE HAZARD. Hydrogen storage at 350-700 bar 
                                (5,000-10,000 psi). Explosive decompression risk. Verify pressure 
                                gauge readings before disconnecting any fitting. Use torque wrench 
                                per torque specifications.

warning
                            
                                HIGH VOLTAGE HAZARD. Fuel cell system operates at ±270 VDC+. 
                                Lethal shock hazard. De-energize system and verify zero voltage 
                                before maintenance. Use insulated tools rated for 600V minimum. 
                                Lock out / tag out required.

warning
                            
                                ASPHYXIATION HAZARD. Hydrogen displaces oxygen. Oxygen 
                                deficient atmosphere can cause unconsciousness without warning. 
                                Monitor oxygen levels continuously in confined spaces. Maintain 
                                minimum 19.5% oxygen concentration. Use breathing apparatus if 
                                oxygen level drops below 19.5%.


### CAUTION

Definition: Indicates a hazard 
                        with a low level of risk which, if not avoided, could result in minor or 
                        moderate injury, or damage to equipment.

Usage Criteria:

Potential for minor injury (cuts, bruises, minor burns)
                            Equipment damage risk
                            Performance degradation
                            Procedural errors with recoverable consequences

| Element | Specification |
| --- | --- |
| Signal Word | CAUTION (all capitals) |
| Color | Yellow background (#FFCC00), Black text |
| Symbol | Triangular hazard symbol with exclamation mark |
| Font Weight | Bold for signal word, regular for message |


Q100-Specific Examples:

caution
                            
                                Do not exceed maximum boil-off rate of 0.5% per day. Excessive 
                                boil-off indicates insulation degradation. Monitor tank pressure and 
                                temperature continuously during ground operations.

caution

                                specified values (15-20 Nm for AN-4 fittings). Over-torquing damages 
                                seals and causes leaks.

Definition: Provides important 
                        information for optimal performance, efficiency, or understanding. Does 
                        not indicate hazard.

Usage Criteria:

Best practices and recommendations
                            Performance optimization tips
                            Explanatory information
                            Cross-references to related procedures

## Placement Rules in S1000D Data Modules

Safety messages must be positioned according to the following rules:

Proximity Rule: Place 
                            warning/caution immediately before the step that presents the hazard.
                        
                        
                            Visibility Rule: Ensure 
                            warning/caution appears on same page as hazardous step (no page breaks).
                        
                        
                            Hierarchy Rule: Multiple 
                            warnings/cautions appear in order: WARNING, then CAUTION, then NOTE.
                        
                        
                            Consolidation Rule: 
                            Multiple warnings of same type may be combined if they relate to the 
                            same step.
                        
                        
                            Front Matter Rule: 
                            General warnings applicable to entire procedure appear in dedicated 
                            "Safety Information" section at beginning of data module.
